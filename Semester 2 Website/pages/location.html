Location

The Perfect Location

Kerry, known as Ireland's Kingdom, is a natural playground. There are so many things to do. Hike through the Killarney National Park, walk on sandy beaches (or surf on the waves!), lunch on the finest Guinness and oysters. We are happy to arrange everything for you to make the most of your stay; golf, sightseeing, walking and hiking, abseiling, rock climbing, kayaking or fishing for wild salmon from the Guesthouse's own private pier. After your day's exploring, wind down with the host of unique complimentary activities that all of our guests enjoy, including pool table, indoor & outdoor vitality pools, heat experiences, Lifestyle showers, Techno Gym & relaxation areas. We also offer complimentary indoor Tennis and Horse riding on site and preferential tee times and fees at Killarney Golf & Fishing Club. Lakeside house is the perfect choice for your break in Kerry. Visitors Guide

Killarney national Park

Ireland's first National Park lies to the south and west of Killarney town at the foot of Ireland's highest mountain range, The MacGillycuddy's Reeks. Killarney National Park contains the most extensive areas of natural woodland remaining in Ireland.

The Gap of Dunloe

The Gap has long been a popular tourist attraction. The road through it is narrow, winding and is difficult for larger vehicles. A popular form of transport for tourists is the horse-drawn trap, a cart where up to four occupants sit facing each other. There are also riding ponies for hire. The trap and ponies are guided by men from families that live in and around the Gap.

Muckross House

Within Killarney National Park is Muckross House a magnificent Victorian mansion and one of Ireland s leading stately homes. The elegantly furnished rooms portray the lifestyles of the landed gentry, while downstairs in the basement one can experience the working conditions of the servants employed in the House.

Ross Castle

Ross Castle sits on the edge of Killarney's lower lake and was built by O'Donoghue Mór in the 15th century. The Castle came into the hands of the Brownes who became the Earls of Kenmare and owned an extensive portion of the lands that are now part of Killarney National Park .
